## 三、While-reading 精读环节（约4.5分钟）

### Part 1: Why should teenagers change bad habits?（约1分钟）

Let's look at **Paragraph 1**.

*(朗读关键句)*

> "These bad habits, if left unchecked, could lead to more serious ones when they become adults."

What does "**if left unchecked**" mean here?

*(稍作停顿)*

It means "如果不加以控制的话"。This is an important structure — it's a **reduced adverbial clause**. The full form would be "if they are left unchecked."

So the author is warning us: bad habits formed during teenage years can become **more serious** in adulthood. For example? 

*(指向文本)*

Tobacco or alcohol abuse — which can lead to physical and mental health problems.

This is why it's **essential** to recognize and change bad habits early.

---

### Part 2: The Habit Cycle（约2分钟）

Now, let's move to the most important part of this article — **The Habit Cycle**.

*(指向图片/板书画出循环图)*

According to modern psychology, every habit works like this:

| Step | English | 中文 | 解释 |
|:----:|:-------:|:----:|:-----|
| 1 | **CUE** | 触发点 | A signal that tells your brain to do something |
| 2 | **ROUTINE** | 惯常行为 | The action you take in response to the cue |
| 3 | **REWARD** | 奖励 | The good feeling you get from the routine |

*(用手势画圈)*

And then the cycle repeats, over and over again, until the behavior becomes automatic — a habit!

Let me give you an example from the text:

*(指向文本关键句)*

> "When we feel unhappy (cue), we eat lots of unhealthy snacks (routine), which makes us feel happy (reward)."

See? 

```
Unhappy → Eat snacks → Feel better → Repeat!
```

That's how a bad habit of relying on unhealthy snacks is formed.

---

### Part 3: How to change bad habits?（约1.5分钟）

So now we understand how habits are formed. But how do we **change** them?

The text tells us: we need to "**facilitate**" a positive change. 

*(板书：facilitate = 促进、使...更容易)*

And the key is: **Don't try to delete the cycle. Instead, REPLACE the routine!**

Look at this example:

> "When we feel unhappy again (cue), we could listen to some of our favourite music instead (routine), which will make us feel relaxed (reward)."

*(用手势强调替换)*

| 要素 | 坏习惯 | 好习惯 |
|:----:|:------:|:------:|
| Cue | Feeling unhappy | Feeling unhappy ✓ |
| Routine | Eating snacks ✗ | Listening to music ✓ |
| Reward | Feeling better | Feeling better ✓ |

The cue stays the same — feeling unhappy.  
The reward stays the same — feeling better.  
But the **routine** changes from eating snacks to listening to music!

This is the secret: **Keep the cue and reward, but change the routine to something positive.**

*(竖起手指强调)*

And remember what the text says about the process of change:

> "The most successful way to change is not suddenly, but over a period of time."

The article quotes the Chinese philosopher **Laozi（老子）**: 

> "A journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step."  
> "千里之行，始于足下。"

This reminds us: change requires **discipline（自律）** and **patience（耐心）**. We must take many small steps repeatedly.

## 四、Post-reading 读后环节（约1分钟）

Now, let's do a quick activity!

Think about **ONE bad habit** you have. It could be staying up late, checking your phone too often, or anything else.

Use the habit cycle to analyze it:

- What's your **CUE**? 
- What's your **ROUTINE**?
- What's your **REWARD**?

And most importantly — what **new routine** could you use to replace the bad one?

*(停顿思考)*

For example, if my bad habit is checking my phone before bed:

| 要素 | 分析 |
|:----:|:-----|
| **Cue** | Lying in bed, feeling bored |
| **Routine** | Scrolling through social media |
| **Reward** | Entertainment, relaxation |
| **New Routine** | Reading a book 📖 |

Same cue, same reward, but a healthier routine!

*(微笑鼓励)*

After class, I want you to complete Activity 4 in your groups and share your ideas.

---

## 五、Summary 总结环节（约0.5分钟）

Let's wrap up what we've learned today.

*(手指板书，逐一总结)*

### 📌 Key Takeaways

1. **Bad teenage habits** can lead to serious problems in adulthood.
2. Habits work in a cycle: **Cue → Routine → Reward**
3. To change a bad habit, we should **replace the routine** with a positive one.
4. Change takes **time and discipline** — one small step at a time!

*(真诚地看向学生)*

Remember, there's no magic pill to change bad habits. But **YOU have the power** to build a happy and healthy life full of good habits!

That's all for today's class. Thank you!

## 🎯 核心词汇 & 语言点

### 重点词汇

| 单词 | 词性 | 释义 | 例句 |
|:-----|:----:|:-----|:-----|
| habit | n. | 习惯 | Bad habits can be hard to change. |
| dominate | v. | 支配、主导 | Don't let bad habits dominate your life. |
| facilitate | v. | 促进、使便利 | We need to facilitate a positive change. |
| discipline | n. | 自律、纪律 | Change requires discipline. |
| pessimistic | adj. | 悲观的 | Don't become pessimistic and give up. |
| reward | n./v. | 奖励 | The reward makes us repeat the behavior. |

### 重点语法

**省略结构（Reduced Adverbial Clause）**

> These bad habits, **if left unchecked**, could lead to more serious ones.

- 完整形式：if they are left unchecked
- 省略条件：从句主语与主句主语一致，且从句含 be 动词
- 类似结构：when asked, while reading, though tired
